W. Larry Dandridge's Biography

November 6, 2025

 Larry Dandridge is married to Judi Dandridge, a retired Special Education and Reading Recovery Specialist. He has five children, nine grandchildren, two great-grandchildren, and a spoiled Italian Greyhound.

 Larry is a Vietnam War wounded warrior; a service-connected and combat-service-connected 100% disabled veteran; an ex-Enlisted Infantryman; an ex-Warrant Officer Attack-Utility-Scout Helicopter/Airplane Pilot, Flight Instructor, Flight Examiner, Test Pilot, Flight Commander, Master Army Aviator, and Airport Manager; a former FAA Certified Flight Instructor (CFI) in Helicopters, Airplanes, and Instruments and Ground Instruction, and an FAA certified Multi and Single Engine Commercial Pilot and a retired Lt. Colonel.

 He is a past VA-accredited Veterans Service Officer (VSO), a Patient Adviser and Patient and Family Centered Care Instructor at his local VA Medical Center, a Fisher House Charleston and Friends of Fisher House Charleston Goodwill Ambassador, a former President and Vice President for Veteran Affairs for the Charleston SC AUSA Chapter, the Service Officer and Veterans Benefits Adviser for the Coastal South Carolina Association of the US Army (AUSA) Chapter, the former Vice President for Veterans/Personal Affairs for the Charleston, SC Chapter of the Military Officers Association of America (MOAA) Chapter, and the Veterans Benefits Advisor for the Cary, NC VFW Post 7383.

 After serving 24 years in the US Army as an Aviator, Logistician, Transportation Officer, a US-Germany Negotiator, an Advisor in Iran, a 120 ton-diesel electric Locomotive Operator, an Aviation Maintenance Company Commander, a Battalion Operations Officer, Division Ground Maintenance Officer, a Procurement Expert, and the Army Aviation Center Chief of Aviation Logistics, Larry worked 20 years as a Program Manager, Lean Six Sigma Black Belt, Hazardous Materials Manager, Quality Manager, Safety Manager, Marketing Manager, Business Process Reengineering Specialist, Proposals Manager, and Regional Manager for QinetiQ North America, Inc., and its legacy companies (Cobro Corporation and Westar Aerospace & Defense Group, Inc.).

 He also founded and served as the Founder, Marketing Manager, Administrator, CEO, and Board Chair of the extraordinarily successful Community Loving Care (CLC) Hospice LLC from 2002 to 2012.

 He taught microeconomics and macroeconomics, technical report writing, aeronautics, business law, and business management part-time at Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University, Chapman University, the University of Maryland, and Lassen Community College. He also served as the Extension Division Manager for Chapman University and Lassen Community College. And he taught business process re-engineering (process mapping, cellular techniques, five-why, mistake proofing, affinity diagramming, Lean Six Sigma, the A-76 process, etc., in industry, at West Point in NY, Ft. Hood, TX, Anniston Army Depot, Ft. Monmouth, NJ, The Naval Support Activity in New Orleans, LA, and the Federal Law Enforcement training Center in GA.

 Larry is an honors graduate of three police academies and served with distinction as a Police Officer in MO and AL (part-time for eight years and one year full-time). His writing on leadership and what citizens should know about a police officer's use of force authority has been widely read.

 Inducted into the West Point Directorate of Logistics Hall of Fame in 2000 and the Boeing and SC RiverDogs Military and the Veterans Hall of Fame in 2024, Larry's awards for military service, public service, and advocacy for veterans are too numerous to list, but include the Legion of Merit, Purple Heart, 15 Air Medals, the Defense Meritorious Service Medal, Five Army Meritorious Service Medals, The Army Commendation Medal, Three Army Silver Quill Awards, The VA Commendation of Meritorious Service; SC Highest Award for Service - the Order of the Palmetto; the SC House of Representatives February 1, 2020, Resolution and Service Award H.5224; and dozens of other Fisher House, AUSA, MOAA, VA, AAAA, VFW, and Army awards.

 Larry is also the author of the award-winning and popular (with over 250 five-star reviews on Amazon, Goodreads, and numerous other sites) Blades of Thunder (Book One), which he wrote to raise money for the Fisher House in Charleston. His speeches, book signings, and book sales have raised over $90,000.00 for Fisher House Charleston. His advocacy for the Fisher House in Charleston catalyzed raising more than $5.5 million to purchase the land on which that remarkable home is built, to refurbish the Fisher House Charleston's historic carriage house, and to operate that miraculous and beautiful home away from home for the families of the patients in the Ralph H. Johnson VA Medical Center.

 Larry is a weekly contributing freelance writer for The Beaufort, SC, ISLAND NEWS Newspaper, a quarterly contributing writer for ALL ABOUT SENIORS Low Country Magazine/Directory, as well as over 20 other magazines and newspapers in the USA, Germany, and England. Larry's award-winning speech and articles on leadership have been used by the US Military Academy at West Point, almost every Army School (Aviation, Field Artillery, Military Police, Adjutant General, Air Defense Artillery, Warrant Officer, and others), the Department of Homeland Security, Blue Cross-Blue Shield of SC, The Military Order of World Wars (MOWW), the National Guard, the Berlin Brigade, Cobro Corporation, the Association of the US Army (AUSA), Westar Aerospace and Defense Group, Inc., the US Army Europe, QinetiQ North America Inc., The German War College (Bundes Praxis Magazine), Lockheed Support Systems Incorporated (LSSI), and other government, business, and patriotic organizations to teach leadership.

 Nowadays, Larry works part-time as a Freelance Writer and a Business Process Reengineering Specialist, with expertise in Lean Six Sigma, Hazardous Materials Management, Marketing, and veterans' benefits consulting. He spends most of his time advocating for military members, veterans, their families, caregivers, and survivors, as well as his local VA Medical Center and Fisher Houses.
